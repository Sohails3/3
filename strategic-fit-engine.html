Strategic Fit
Engine

An AI-powered M&A target screening workflow built in the GP Bullhound house style. Input a buyer, a sector, and a geography — the pipeline researches the buyer's acquisition history, discovers 8–10 private targets, scores them across 7 criteria, and outputs an interactive investment banking report with a live DCF valuation model.

// How it works

A 4-Step AI Pipeline

Three inputs drive the entire workflow — buyer name, target sector, and geography. Claude handles the research and reasoning; deterministic Python handles the scoring, maths, and output.

STEP 01

Buyer DNA Analysis

Claude researches the buyer's last 5–7 acquisitions, strategic priorities, and product gaps — then derives 4 buyer-specific scoring criteria.

STEP 02

Target Discovery

Identifies 8–10 real private companies in the target sector and geography with verified financial and operational data.

STEP 03

Strategic Fit Scoring

Each company is scored across 7 criteria — 4 buyer-specific and 3 universal M&A criteria. Totals are recalculated in Python, never trusted from the model.

STEP 04

GP Bullhound Report

Generates an interactive HTML dashboard with SVG charts, per-company acquisition briefs, a live DCF model with editable assumptions, and CSV export.

// Output

What the Report Contains

  • Executive summary with KPI cards and ranked company score chart
  • Buyer profile — acquisition history, strategic priorities, scoring rubric
  • Sortable, filterable target screening table across 7 scoring criteria
  • Top 3 acquisition reports in GP Bullhound Transaction Snapshot format — recommendation, deal metrics, synergy analysis, risk table, integration roadmap
  • DCF valuation with editable WACC, growth rate, and margin assumptions — live recalculation in the browser
  • Radar chart comparing top 3 targets across all 7 criteria
  • CSV export of the full DCF model

// Stack

Technical Architecture

Built on the WAT framework — Workflows, Agents, Tools — separating AI reasoning from deterministic execution to maintain reliability across chained steps.
